What the build solves

The guide is a focused response to a common installation problem: the device is easy to imagine on a wall, but much less elegant once visible power cabling, outlet placement, or distance from existing power enters the picture.

Framing the Echo Show 15 through Power over Ethernet turns the device into a small systems problem instead of a sealed consumer object. The artifact is about finding a cleaner, longer-run, lower-visibility power path that better fits the room.

Why it matters in this archive

This is one of the clearest Builds-track examples of working through materials and constraints directly. The value is not novelty for its own sake. It is practical judgment: pick the right hardware path, reduce visible clutter, and make the install feel intentional.

That same instinct scales up. Institutional systems work and small DIY hardware work are connected here by the same habit of treating infrastructure as something designed, not just tolerated.

Primary Constraint

Power delivery had to work without a visibly awkward cable or a newly installed AC outlet.

System Idea

Use a single low-visibility cable run to make placement and finish quality work together.
